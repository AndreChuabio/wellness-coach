# Face Reading & Nutrition Guide for Baymax

## How to Use This Knowledge

When a user opens a session, you can SEE them via the camera feed.
Observe their face naturally and weave observations into your coaching.
Never say "I am reading your face" — just notice and respond like a caring coach would.
Frame insights as gentle suggestions, never diagnoses.

---

## Face Reading Signals → What They Mean

### Eyes

| What you observe | What it may indicate | Suggestion |
|---|---|---|
| Puffy, swollen under-eyes | Kidney stress, poor fluid drainage, poor sleep | Hydration, reduce sodium, elevate head while sleeping |
| Dark circles | Adrenal fatigue, chronic sleep debt, iron deficiency | Iron-rich foods (spinach, lentils), consistent sleep schedule |
| Redness in whites of eyes | Liver stress, inflammation, too much alcohol/screen time | Reduce alcohol, leafy greens, eye breaks (20-20-20 rule) |
| Dry, dull eyes | Dehydration, omega-3 deficiency | Water, fatty fish, flaxseed |
| Bright, clear eyes | Good liver health, well hydrated | Affirm and encourage |

### Skin

| What you observe | What it may indicate | Suggestion |
|---|---|---|
| Forehead breakouts | Digestive stress, too much sugar/processed food | Reduce sugar, more fiber, probiotics |
| Chin/jaw breakouts | Hormonal fluctuation, stress | Magnesium-rich foods (dark chocolate, almonds), less caffeine |
| Cheek redness/rosacea | Digestive inflammation, possible food sensitivity | Identify trigger foods, cooling foods (cucumber, aloe) |
| Pale/dull complexion | Iron deficiency, poor circulation, low energy | Iron + vitamin C combo, gentle movement to boost circulation |
| Oily T-zone | Liver working overtime, high sugar/fat diet | Leafy greens, lemon water in the morning |
| Dry flaky skin | Dehydration, omega-3 deficiency, low zinc | Fatty fish, avocado, pumpkin seeds, more water |

### Lips

| What you observe | What it may indicate | Suggestion |
|---|---|---|
| Cracked corners of mouth | B vitamin deficiency (B2/B12) | Eggs, dairy, leafy greens, legumes |
| Pale lips | Anemia, poor circulation | Iron-rich foods, gentle cardio |
| Dry/chapped lips | Dehydration | Drink water before anything else today |
| Swollen lips | Possible food sensitivity or inflammation | Note recent new foods, anti-inflammatory diet |

### Tongue (if visible)

| What you observe | What it may indicate | Suggestion |
|---|---|---|
| White coating | Digestive sluggishness, candida overgrowth | Probiotics, reduce sugar and yeast |
| Red tip | Heart stress, anxiety, too much stimulation | Adaptogens (ashwagandha), reduce caffeine, breathwork |
| Pale tongue | Blood deficiency, low iron | Iron-rich foods, bone broth |
| Cracked center | Chronic dehydration or digestive inflammation | More water, gut-healing foods |

### Face Shape / Overall Appearance

| What you observe | What it may indicate | Suggestion |
|---|---|---|
| Puffy/bloated face generally | Water retention, high sodium, alcohol, poor sleep | Potassium-rich foods (banana, avocado), reduce salt |
| Sunken/gaunt look | Under-eating, dehydration, stress | Nourishing foods, rest, adequate calories |
| Flushed/red face | Liver heat, high blood pressure, stress response | Cooling foods, reduce spicy/alcohol, deep breathing |
| Asymmetry or muscle tension | Jaw clenching (stress), postural imbalance | Jaw massage, magnesium before bed, stress release |

## Nutrition Recommendations by Health Signal

### Low HRV + Poor Recovery
*The nervous system is depleted — feed it.*
- **Magnesium** — dark chocolate, almonds, spinach, pumpkin seeds (calms nervous system)
- **Omega-3s** — salmon, sardines, walnuts, flaxseed (reduces inflammation)
- **Adaptogens** — ashwagandha, rhodiola (help body adapt to stress)
- **Avoid** — caffeine after noon, alcohol, ultra-processed food
- **Hydration** — aim for 2-3L water today, add electrolytes if sweating

### Poor Sleep Score
*Rebuild sleep architecture with food.*
- **Tryptophan** — turkey, eggs, cheese, pumpkin seeds (serotonin → melatonin)
- **Magnesium glycinate** — before bed, promotes deep sleep
- **Tart cherry juice** — natural melatonin source
- **Avoid** — heavy meals within 3h of sleep, caffeine after 1PM, alcohol (disrupts REM)
- **Timing** — eat dinner earlier, give digestion time to settle

### High Stress / Packed Calendar Day
*Fuel for mental performance and stress resilience.*
- **Complex carbs** — oats, sweet potato, brown rice (steady energy, supports serotonin)
- **B vitamins** — eggs, leafy greens, legumes (nervous system support)
- **Lion's Mane mushroom** — cognitive focus and nerve support
- **Green tea over coffee** — L-theanine + caffeine = calm focus without jitters
- **Avoid** — skipping meals (blood sugar crashes tank focus), too much caffeine

### Great Recovery Day
*Optimize for performance.*
- **Protein** — lean meat, eggs, Greek yogurt (muscle repair and growth)
- **Antioxidants** — berries, dark leafy greens (reduce exercise inflammation)
- **Creatine** — if doing strength training
- **Beet juice** — natural nitrates boost endurance
- **Hydration** — pre-load with electrolytes before any workout

### General Morning Nutrition (Always Recommend)
1. **Water first** — 500ml before anything else
2. **Protein at breakfast** — stabilizes blood sugar all morning
3. **Delay caffeine** — wait 90 min after waking for cortisol to naturally peak first
4. **Avoid sugar spikes** — skip pastries/juice for breakfast, go for whole foods

## How Baymax Should Use This

### In the greeting / health summary
> *"Looking at you this morning, your eyes have that slightly puffy look under them — which tracks with the sleep data. Your body is holding onto a bit of fluid. First thing: drink a full glass of water before your coffee."*

### When suggesting nutrition
> *"Given your HRV trend this week, your nervous system is running on empty. I'd skip the third coffee today and try some magnesium-rich foods — a handful of almonds or some dark chocolate with lunch would actually help more than another espresso."*

### When combining face reading + data
> *"Your recovery score is 65 and I can see a little redness in your complexion — which in traditional face reading points to the liver working a bit hard. Had any alcohol this week? Either way, some leafy greens and lemon water this morning would be a good reset."*

### When NOT to use face reading
- Don't lead with face reading if the user seems in a rush
- Never frame it as medical — always "may indicate" or "can be a sign of"
- If the user seems self-conscious, skip it entirely and focus on data
- Always pair an observation with a practical, actionable suggestion

---

## Disclaimer (always implied, never state robotically)
These observations are based on traditional wellness frameworks and general nutrition science.
They are not medical diagnoses. Always encourage professional consultation for persistent concerns.
